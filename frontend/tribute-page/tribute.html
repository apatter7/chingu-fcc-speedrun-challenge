"I don't listen to what art critics say. I don't know anybody who needs a critic to find out what art is"- Jean-Michel Basquiat

Jean-Michel Basquiat in his Great Jones Street studio, New York, 1987

Here is a timelife of his life and work:

  • 1960- Born December 22 in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1964- Developed an interest in art and drawing at an early age, drawing frequently and took trips with his mother to The Brooklyn Museum, the MoMA and the Met
  • 1967- Attends St. Ann’s, a private Catholic School where he meets Marc Prozzo. They create a children’s books together
  • 1968- Basquiat is hit by an automobile breaking his arm and causing various internal injuries and had to have his spleen removed. During his month long recovery he received a copy of Gray’s Anatomy from his mother. This influence is found in Basquiat’s later work
  • 1976- Begins attending City-as-School a progressive school in Manhattan. He meets Al Diaz a graffitist. They became best friends and early artistic collaborators
  • 1977- Basquiat becomes involved with an Upper West Side drama group called Family Life Theatre. Here he creates a fiction character named SAMO (Same Old Shit) which sparks a new project for Basquiat and Diaz
  • 1979- Basquiat and Diaz have a falling out that ends the SAMO collaboration- “SAMO is dead”
  • 1980- Basquiat’s art is publically exhibited for the first time in the “Times Square Show”
  • 1983- Basquiat and Warhol work together, painting each other’s portraits, and attending events
  • 1985- Sixteen collaborative paintings by Basquiat and Warhol are shown at the Tony Shafrazi Gallery
  • 1987- Warhol dies devastating Basquiat
  • 1988-Dies on August 12 at the age of 27
